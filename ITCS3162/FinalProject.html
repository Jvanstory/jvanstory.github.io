Final Project: Inflation

Introduction

Over time, the cost of goods and services has been on the upward trend, contributing to a continuous rise in inflation. This is a significant concern because, as prices escalate, an increasing number of individuals may find it challenging to afford essential items. In light of this, my project aims to develop a predictive model for the Consumer Price Index (CPI). The CPI serves as a crucial metric, representing the average change over time in the prices paid by urban consumers for a specified market basket of consumer goods. The objective is to enhance our understanding and prediction of how these price fluctuations impact the overall cost of living.


Introduce the data

I am utilizing the "U.S. Inflation Data" dataset sourced from Kaggle. This dataset consists of two key columns: one containing the year and month, and the other representing the Consumer Price Index (CPI). The CPI values provide the average measure of price changes for a specified market basket of consumer goods across all U.S. cities. Spanning from 1912 to 2021, this dataset offers a comprehensive historical perspective on inflation, allowing for a thorough analysis and modeling of the fluctuations in the cost of living over time.


Methods

The data preprocessing steps I implemented involved removing rows containing missing data and splitting the dataset into training and test sets. I opted for an 80-20 split, allocating 80% of the data for training and the remaining portion for testing. Given the simplicity of my dataset, minimal preprocessing was necessary. Initially, I selected an ARIMA model for its suitability in analyzing and forecasting time series data. However, due to unsatisfactory results, I decided to explore an alternative model. Subsequently, I adopted the naive forecast model, which produced significantly improved outcomes. I chose this model for its simplicity, ease of understanding, and effectiveness when dealing with data closely related in value to the previous observations. Considering the relatively gradual changes in the Consumer Price Index (CPI) on a daily basis, I deemed the naive forecast model a fitting choice.
